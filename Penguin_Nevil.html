Introduction

The Palmer Penguins dataset contains measurements collected from penguins living in the Palmer Archipelago of Antarctica. The dataset includes three penguin species: Adelie, Chinstrap, and Gentoo. The penguins were observed on Biscoe, Dream, and Torgersen islands.

The dataset includes information such as bill length, bill depth, flipper length, body mass, sex, species, island, and year.

In this project, I will examine whether males and females are represented equally, whether the species and islands are represented equally, which measurements help distinguish males from females, which measurements help distinguish the species, and which species were observed on each island.

Data Visualization

The following visualizations explore differences among penguin sexes, species, and island locations.## Male vs Female Body Mass

The boxplot below compares the body mass of male and female penguins.

penguins %>%
  filter(!is.na(sex), !is.na(body_mass_g)) %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = sex, y = body_mass_g, fill = sex)) +
  geom_boxplot() +
  facet_wrap(~species) +
  labs(
    title = "Body Mass of Male and Female Penguins",
    x = "Sex",
    y = "Body Mass (g)"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

## Species Comparison

The scatterplot below compares bill length and bill depth among the three penguin species.

penguins %>%
  filter(!is.na(bill_length_mm), !is.na(bill_depth_mm)) %>%
  ggplot(aes(
    x = bill_length_mm,
    y = bill_depth_mm,
    color = species
  )) +
  geom_point(size = 3) +
  labs(
    title = "Bill Length vs Bill Depth",
    x = "Bill Length (mm)",
    y = "Bill Depth (mm)"
  ) +
  theme_classic()

## Penguin Species by Island

The bar chart below shows which penguin species were observed on each island.

penguins %>%
  count(island, species) %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = island, y = n, fill = species)) +
  geom_col(position = "dodge") +
  labs(
    title = "Penguin Species by Island",
    x = "Island",
    y = "Number of Penguins"
  ) +
  theme_light()

# Conclusion

The Palmer Penguins dataset contains measurements from 344 penguins across three species and three islands. The analysis showed that males and females are represented in similar numbers, although not exactly equally. The species and islands are also not represented equally.

Body mass is useful for distinguishing male and female penguins, while bill measurements help distinguish among Adelie, Chinstrap, and Gentoo penguins. The visualizations also show how the three species are distributed across the islands included in the dataset.
